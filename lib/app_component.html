Characters

Ammon - A 20-30 something Nephite
Aaron - A 20-30 something Nephite
Omner - A 20-30 something Nephite
Himi - A 20-30 something Nephite
Abish - An 18 year old Lamanite
Gokkcha'ar* - A 24 year old Lamanite servant to King Lamoni and love interest of Abish
Samuel - Gokkcha'ar's cousin, an older Lamanite servant to King Lamoni who secretly loves Lorhah
Lorhah - An older Lamanite and friend of Abish and servant of the Queen who secretly loves Samuel
Lamoni - King of the land where Ammon goes to preach
The Queen - King Lamoni's wife

*Note that some names (particularly Klingon sounding ones) are placeholders

Song Titles and Synopsis

ACT I

Cursed/The Sons of Mosiah (The Nephites)
Chorus number setting up the story that the sons of Mosiah are going to a filthy, vile, hated people to preach the gospel to them.

Take Courage and Go Forth (Ammon, Aaron, Omner, Himni)
The sons of Mosiah prepare for their visit

Wronged (The Lamanites, Ammon, Aaron, Omner, Himni)
The Lamanites tell of how they were wronged by their forefather's brethren, the forefathers of the Nephites. The sons of Mosiah try and preach to them and are rejected repeatedly.

The Custom (Gokkcha’ar, Abish, Lamanite Servant)
Gokkcha'ar and Abish have a bit of a tiff about Gokkcha'ar's new assignment to watch the king's sheep where it is well know that robbers scatter the sheep and then the king has his servants killed. They also debate the truth of God, which Gokkcha'ar does not believe Ammon arrives and Gokkcha'ar and some other servants grab him, bind him and take him to the king.

The Vision (Abish)
Abish tells of her vision from God of His existence and of Christ's.

Weary World (Lamoni)
The king laments about the lack of joy in the world but has hope that there's something better.

Kneel Before Your King (The First, Lamoni, Gokkcha’ar)
Lamoni is honored by his servants and attendants. Gokkcha'ar brings Ammon before him. Lamoni asks Ammon what he desires.

‘Til the Day I Die (Ammon)
Ammon explains that his desire is to dwell among the Lamanites, perhaps until the day he dies.

What Will Be Tonight (Abish, Samuel, Lorhah)
Abish and Lorhah meet with Samuel on his way for the nightly watch of the sheep. Abish tells him to take care of Gokkcha'ar. He tells her that what will be will be and they simply had to do their duties. He and Lorhah exchange greetings.

Could You Love Me Too (Samuel and Lorhah)
Samuel and Lorhah separately sing of their love for one another

The Sun (Samuel)
Samuel at the night watch tells of his hope that the morning will come without incident, using the Sun as an allegory of good things

It Was Amazing (Gokkcha’ar)
The next morning in Lamoni's court Gokkcha'ar enters and tells of Ammon's amazing feats in defeating the robbers and in cutting off their arms, and how he could not be killed.

The Great Spirit (Lamoni, Ammon)
Ammon comes before Lamoni. The king asks him by what power he does what he does. Ammon asks him if he knows God. He says he does not. Ammon asks him if he believes in the great spirit. The king does.

Behold, This is God (Ammon)
Ammon explains God to king Lamoni.

Yea, I Believe (Lamoni, Ammon)
The king believe all of Ammons words.

Lay Me Down (Lamoni, Servants)
The king falls into a trance.

To Me He Doth Not Stink (The Queen, Servants)
Days later, the queen's servants are telling her that the king, still in his trance, is dead and that he stinks. The queen doesn't believe he stinks.

Blessed Be the Name of God/Yea, I Believe (reprise) (The Queen, Lamoni, Ammon, Servants)
The queen calls for Ammon and asks if the king is dead. Ammon raises the king and Lamoni testifies to the queen.

Lay Me Down (reprise) (The Queen, Lamoni, Ammon, Servants)
The queen, the king, Ammon, and all the household, including Lorhah, Samuel, and Gokkcha'ar are converted to the Lord and fall into a trance.

Beyond This Slumber (Abish)
Abish expresses her joy that others now know of the truth of God.

From House to House/Multitude (Abish, Lamanites)
Abish runs from house to house to tell everyone of the miracle.

Monster (Lamanites)
The gathered Lamanites at the king's court argue about the nature of Ammon and the state of those in the trance.

As I Hold Your Hand (Abish)
Abish raises those in a trance by touching their hands.

From the Fountain of His Love (Abish, Gokkcha’ar)
Abish and Gokkcha'ar sing of their strengthened love and their trust and believe in God.
